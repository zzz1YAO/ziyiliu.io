About Me

I am an incoming PhD student in Artificial Intelligence at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou), where I will be supervised by Prof. Hao Liu. Before that, I studied Artificial Intelligence at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT).

Broadly, I am interested in multimodal reasoning, long video understanding, and large language model based agents. I enjoy building systems that can perceive complex visual streams, preserve useful memory over long horizons, and coordinate tools or sub-agents to solve difficult tasks.

My goal is to build multimodal systems that reason more reliably over long temporal contexts, especially where video, language, and decision making intersect.

Research Interests

Multimodal Reasoning

Building models that reason across video, image, and text to make grounded and interpretable decisions.

Long-Video Understanding

Designing memory, retrieval, and planning mechanisms for extremely long temporal contexts.

LLM-Based Agents

Studying how language models can orchestrate perception, tool use, and collaboration in multimodal systems.
